# Platform Safety

Use this reference when a script contains injury, violence, blood, poisoning, mutation, experiments, weapons, explosions, self-harm, torture, or other audit-sensitive content.

These rules are universal platform-safety rules. Do not add them to every generic prompt unless the current story content needs them.

## Core Rule

Keep characters as real human actors with physically plausible bodies and restrained live-action performance. Replace risky visuals with safe visual evidence, reactions, props, staging, and consequences. Do not simply delete high-risk story information if it affects plot.

## Injury and Blood

Avoid direct gore:

- No spraying blood, open wounds, exposed tissue, organs, dismemberment, wound-healing close-ups, or graphic injury detail.
- Prefer mouth-corner trace, clothing stain, gauze, bruise, dark mark, sweat, stagger, hand pressing a covered area, breath changing, needing support, or losing balance.
- Show outcome and reaction rather than injury mechanics.

## Poisoning, Mutation, and Loss of Control

Keep a human scale:

- Eyes can be bloodshot, unfocused, wet, or rimmed red; do not use glowing monster eyes.
- Mouth movement stays within normal human range; do not write exaggerated monster mouth or fangs unless the project explicitly requires non-realistic creatures.
- Use tremor, unstable steps, scratching at collar, clenched jaw, breathing shift, delayed reaction, or inability to focus.
- Fog, poison, aura, or abnormal energy should be environmental pressure, not monster transformation spectacle.

## Violence, Fights, Weapons

- Fight scenes need start, contact or near-contact, force direction, result, and opponent reaction.
- Use realistic weight, distance, blocking, slipping, covering, dodging, falling behind objects, and bystander reactions.
- Avoid superhuman flight, body deformation, excessive impact, graphic hits, or lingering injury detail.
- Weapons can create pressure through distance, sound, stance, lowered muzzle/blade, cover, and reaction. Avoid explicit harm results unless safely implied.

## Experiments and Coercion

- Use equipment, restraints, gloves, trays, glassware, files, monitors, doors, distance, and power position.
- Avoid injection close-ups, body damage, surgery detail, organ detail, or prolonged suffering display.
- If the plot needs a result, show aftermath: weakened posture, changed breathing, restrained hands, spilled liquid, broken device, or dialogue consequence.
- Do not use forced intimate contact as a generic way to interrupt dialogue. If the source script truly requires coercive intimacy, reduce the image to safe blocking, cutaway, breath stopping, body pulling back, hand blocking, distance change, or aftermath reaction; avoid eroticized detail and prolonged contact.

## Safe Prompt Supplements

Add a scene-specific safety supplement only when needed:

```text
受伤和失控画面保持真人短剧尺度，只用纱布、衣料痕迹、身体反应、站位压迫和角色反应承载，不表现高刺激细节。
```

```text
打斗动作真实受力，写清起点、发力方向、阻挡物、结果和对方反应，不做夸张特效或违反人体物理的动作。
```

```text
实验风险用设备、文件、束带、玻璃器皿、门口站位和台词后果承载，不展示直接伤害过程。
```

## Coverage Requirement

When unsafe content is reduced, record internally what replaced it:

- Original risk information.
- Safe visual expression.
- Preserved plot function.
- Next action or relationship consequence.

If the replacement does not preserve the plot function, return to director design.
